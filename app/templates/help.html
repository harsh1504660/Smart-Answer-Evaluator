Marking Formula

Welcome to the help section of the Smart Answer Evaluator. Here, we explain how our platform calculates the marks for student answers based on the defined ranges and thresholds.

The Smart Answer Evaluator uses a sophisticated marking algorithm to ensure fair and accurate grading. The algorithm works as follows:

1. Define Ranges

We start by defining the ranges for correct, partial, and incorrect answers based on the maximum marks:

  • Correct: 50% to 100% of maximum marks
  • Partial: 40% to 60% of maximum marks
  • Incorrect: 0% to 40% of maximum marks

2. Threshold Probabilities

We set threshold probabilities for soft and hard checking:

  • Soft Checking:
    • Correct: 0.9
    • Partial: 0.6
    • Incorrect: 0.75
  • Hard Checking:
    • Correct: 0.95
    • Partial: 0.65
    • Incorrect: 0.7

3. Calculate Marks

The marks are calculated based on the predicted class's probability and its threshold:

  1. If the predicted class's probability is equal to or above the threshold, the maximum marks for that class's range are awarded.
  2. If the predicted class does not meet the threshold, marks are reduced based on the distance from the threshold:
    • Difference: Calculate the difference between the threshold and the predicted probability.
    • Distance: Divide the difference by a reduction step to determine the distance.
    • Reduction Factor: Calculate the reduction factor as 10% of the maximum marks.
    • Total Reduction: Multiply the distance by the reduction factor to get the total reduction.
    • Marks: Subtract the total reduction from the lower limit of the range to get the final marks.

For example, if the predicted partial probability is below the partial threshold in soft checking:

difference = PARTIAL_THRESHOLD_SOFT - p_partial
distance = round(difference / REDUCTION_STEP )
reduction_factor = max_marks * 0.1
total_reduction = distance * reduction_factor
marks = custom_round(partial_range_min - total_reduction)

This ensures a fair and balanced approach to grading, accounting for varying degrees of answer accuracy.
